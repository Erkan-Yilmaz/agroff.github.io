Reddcoin Full-chain and SPV Wallets

Reddcoin Project officially provides two types of wallets: full-chain and SPV.

The Reddcoin Core full-chain wallet is a full-chain desktop wallet that traces its root back to the official Bitcoin Core wallet and continues to incorporate the latest features from the Bitcoin source code. The wallet provides both command line daemons and QT-based GUI for Windows, Linux and Mac OS X. The full-chain wallet keeps a local copy of the entire blockchain whose size grows continuously.

The Reddcoin social wallet is another full-chain desktop wallet that adopts a fresh new design for user interface and integrates various social features such as real time price conversion, Reddit feeds, Twitter feeds and an IRC client.

SPV stands for Simplified Payment Verification, which was first introduced in section 8 of the original Bitcoin whitepaper. Reddcoin SPV wallets use the Electrum protocol and only need to keep track of block headers and addresses with non-zero outstanding balances. Reddcoin Electrum wallets are currently under active development and will come in three flavours:

  • The Reddcoin Electrum desktop wallets for Windows, Linux and Mac OS X.
  • The Reddcoin Electrum mobile wallets for iOS and Android devices.
  • The Reddcoin Electrum browser wallets for Chrome, Safari and Firefox.

Reddcoin Electrum Features

Instant
Your wallet doesn't download the entire blockchain but communicates with a network of specialised servers. It's usually ready for use within seconds.
Forgiving
Your wallet can be fully recovered anywhere from one secret seed. There is no more need for backing up wallet files like wallet.dat
Safe
Your seed and private keys are encrypted and stored locally on your device only.
Low trust
Data received from servers are verified using SPV. Servers are authenticated using SSL.
No downtime
Your client can automatically switch to the most reliable servers.
Ubiquitous
You can use one seed for multiple wallets on multiple devices and they will stay in sync automatically.
Cold Storage
You can keep all private keys in cold storage and still safely transact on an online computer.
Compatible
Your private keys can be exported to all the other Reddcoin wallets.

Reddcoin Electrum FAQs

What is a Seed?
Reddcoin Electrum wallets use a 128-bit random seed to generate your private keys. The seed can be represented as a 12-word passphrase which is easy to write down or remember. Regular backups are no longer necessary because all of your addresses and balances can be recovered anywhere from this single passphrase.
What is the gap limit?
The gap limit is the maximum number of consecutive unused addresses in your deterministic sequence of addresses. Reddcoin Electrum wallets use a gap limit of 5 by default, so they generate new addresses until 5 unused addresses are found.
What does it mean to "freeze" an address?
When you freeze an address, Reddcoins in that address cannot be sent.
What should I do with my old Reddcoin addresses when switching to Reddcoin Electrum wallets?
The best way to switch to Reddcoin Electrum wallets is to send all your Reddcoins to one of your Reddcoin Electrum addresses. This way you'll have all your Reddcoins secured with your seed and fully recovered anywhere with it. Keep in mind that sending all your Reddcoins will reset their coin age to zero and be a minor temporary setback for staking.
Where is my wallet file located on my PC?
A default wallet file called default_wallet is created under the reddcoin-wallets folder when you first run a Reddcoin Electrum desktop wallet. On Windows, this folder is located in \Users\YourUserName\AppData\Roaming\Local\Reddcoin-Electrum. On all other operating systems, it is located in ~/.reddcoin-electrum.
Can I use Reddcoin Electrum wallets on my smart phone?
Reddcoin Electrum mobile wallets are currently under active development and will be available soon.
